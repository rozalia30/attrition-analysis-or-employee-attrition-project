# Employee Attrition Analysis — HR Analytics Case Study

## 📌 Why This Project
Employee turnover is one of the most expensive problems HR teams face — every departure costs time, money, and institutional knowledge. With a background in HR coordination, I wanted to explore what data-driven insights could reveal about *why* employees leave, and how that could support smarter retention decisions.

This project uses the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset to identify the key factors linked to attrition and turn them into practical recommendations.

## ❓ Business Questions
1. Which departments and job roles have the highest attrition rates?
2. Does overtime correlate with employees leaving?
3. Is there a pay gap between employees who stay vs. those who leave?
4. How do job satisfaction and work-life balance relate to attrition?

## 🛠 Tools & Methods
- **Python** (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) in Google Colab
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Data cleaning and grouping
- Visualizations: bar charts, heatmaps, boxplots
